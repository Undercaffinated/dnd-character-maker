In D&D, at levels 4, 8, 12, 16, and 19, you get some nifty bonuses along with your normal level up. At each of these levels, you are awarded two "Ability Score Bonus Points" which can be added to whichever Ability Score you desire. The only rule is that you are not allowed to go above 20 this way.

Because D&D hates you, there is exactly one exception. Fighters get an additional 2 bonus points at levels 6 and 14. Don't ask me why. It's probably because fighters suck.

For example, a level 5 character is at least level 4, so they get 2 Bonus Points. A level 17 character gets 8.

Since you are level ##, you get ## bonus points to add to your ability scores which you can assign any way you want. You may also exchange 2 bonus points for a feat instead. If you do this, don't forget to add your new feat to your character sheet!
